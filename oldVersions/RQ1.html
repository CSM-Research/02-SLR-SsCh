[21] Long-living software systems are sustainable if they can be cost-efficiently maintained and evolved over their entire lifecycle. The quality of software architectures determines sustainability to a large extent. A software-intensive system is long-living if it must be operated for more than 15 years. The opposite of a sustainable software system is a longliving system that cannot be adapted to changing requirements and environments due to unjustifiable costs or even technical infeasibility. The architecture of a sustainable system may evolve during its life-cycle, but the fulfillment of customer requirements within timing, budget, and quality constraints must be assured.
